I was 14 when I released the first version of Blueprint. I'm 17 now. That's a solid three years.

Blueprint has been growing ever since the first release, going from "just a random project" to a [fisically sponsored nonprofit structure](https://hcb.hackclub.com/blueprint) last year. We're also the home to the best-sold Pterodactyl theme, [Nebula](https://nebula.style).

### Questions and answers

I asked our community to come up with a few questions. Here are my answers!

#### Why did you start Blueprint?

I started Blueprint together with [Jack](https://github.com/sp11rum), though they now remain as part of the moderation team. Blueprint was made to free Pterodactyl themes and add-ons by making installation of them easy, and preventing conflicts between them.

It first launched under a paid structure, but within a month, we pivoted to becoming free and open source.

#### How long do you plan to keep Blueprint running?

As long as I can! To this day, I'm still motivated to work on this platform, and I've got plenty of long-term ideas to continue for the next three years.

#### What is the main goal of Blueprint?

I'd say Blueprint has a few goals:

- Introduce new talent to the game-hosting industry. We do this through guides, documentation, tooling and other resources.
- Serve as the primary way for developers to create Pterodactyl modifications.
- Serve as the primary way for system administrators to install and manage Pterodactyl modifications.

Pterodactyl is currently our main focus, but long-term we seek to expand into other game-hosting-related areas.

#### What is the reason to choose Blueprint over standalone Pterodactyl?

It's personal taste, really. While I don't really get why people would spend the amount of hassle they have to do for standalone modifications, they seem to be happy doing so.

Blueprint is for the people that value their time, ones that prefer convienience over complexity, backed by our free and open source extension framework.

#### What is Blueprint's end-game if Pterodactyl stops?

We're throwing around the idea of working on an alternative to Pterodactyl, fundamentally different to what's already on the market, focused on extensibility.

Pterodactyl doesn't have to end for this to become reality, and Blueprint's support and development on our Pterodactyl-side of things won't end.

#### What will Blueprint's next big thing be?

We're planning to launch a on-site marketplace for purchasing and downloading extensions, backed by our organization, and not struggling with the same pitfalls as other marketplaces have.

#### How do you feel about how Blueprint has evolved over time?

Blueprint, as it is today, is so much better than it was when it just began. That said, we're just getting started, and I'm looking towards the next three years.
