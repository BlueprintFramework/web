Yesterday, [Dane](https://github.com/DaneEveritt) stepped down from Pterodactyl [(source)](https://discord.com/channels/122900397965705216/124919575534895105/1509661235010867340):

::card
Popping in to drop a quick bombshell in the villa today —

<u>**I want let everyone know that I will be stepping down from my maintainer and administrator roles within this project, effective immediately.**</u> Before you start panicking — this is not because I do not believe in this project, nor is it because of any issues with the wonderful folks at Infraly! I have accepted a new job in the gaming industry and will be stepping down from Pterodactyl to focus all of my attention there and to avoid any potential conflicts of interest.

Pterodactyl is still near and dear to my heart, and I will continue to be present in the community. I trust the Infraly team to continue to be good stewards of the project and community. Who knows, maybe you'll finally see me back in Farming Simulator for 7-hours straight on a random weekday night?

So long, and thanks for all the fish.

Dane

This came as a surprise to many people in the Pterodactyl and Blueprint community. Not even twenty minutes later, a new announcement was posted.. [(source)](https://discord.com/channels/122900397965705216/124919575534895105/1509665635611705524)

::card
Hello my fellow egg enthusiasts, as of the recent announcement, I am currently the developer at Infraly that will be taking over the development of Pterodactyl.

A little bit about me: I've been tinkering with code for about ten years, and have worked professionally in the AI / networking field for five of them! I've been a long time lover of Pterodactyl, and I'm very excited to work on a project that I've watched closely since 2018!

Feel free to @ me for any questions, I'd be happy to give my thoughts on what I'd like to take on next :)

\[Robert\]

[Robert](https://github.com/robertdrakedennis)? Who is Robert and what are they doing with our Pterodactyl?

### The comeback

Infraly took over Pterodactyl development earlier this year, with Dane reclaiming the role of lead maintainer, after stepping down in 2022.

WestCoastWill from Infraly promised "long-term stability, consistent maintenance and reliable support" and a "long-awaited security and bug fix update in the coming days", [(source)](https://discord.com/channels/122900397965705216/124919575534895105/1452072860762898524) and Dane promised "pushing out ~~new bugs~~ new features for everyone to enjoy". [(source)](https://discord.com/channels/122900397965705216/124919575534895105/1452072878781501441)

They released panel version `v1.12.0` on January 6th 2026. Over the months that followed, 3 other panel updates were released: `v1.12.1`, `v1.12.2` and `v1.12.3`. These released fixed a few bugs and vulnerabilities.

These updates were just focused on maintenance, no new features, no new website, just a few changes to the Discord server's channel organization.

#### The obvious elephant in the room

Dane leaving has now caused the project to be fully under leadership of Infraly LLC, a for-profit company primarily in the game-hosting space.

This creates the biggest conflict of interest we've ever seen in the Pterodactyl space. Keeping Pterodactyl development stagnated directly profits Infraly in 2 ways:

- Infraly owns a paid Pterodactyl alternative, Wisp. [(source)](https://wisp.gg/) Stagnating Pterodactyl development would make Wisp a better-looking alternative for Pterodactyl, generating them more revenue.
- Infraly owns a hosting provider, Physgun. Stagnating Pterodactyl development would make Physgun's control panel farther ahead of other game-hosting providers'.

### Who is Robert?

Personally, I hadn't seen them around in the Pterodactyl community before, but they seem to work for Infraly and will be taking over the development of Pterodactyl.

They wrote an introduction when announcing taking over Pterodactyl development:

> I've been tinkering with code for about ten years, and have worked professionally in the AI / networking field for five of them! I've been a long time lover of Pterodactyl, and I'm very excited to work on a project that I've watched closely since 2018!

According to messages in the Pterodactyl Discord Robert will be taking over Pterodactyl development [(source)](https://discord.com/channels/122900397965705216/124919575534895105/1509665635611705524), adding functionality and releasing security fixes.
